**TITLE:** AI-Powered Discussion Monitor and Action Item Tracker


**Work Done:**
This week, our team focused on customer interaction and requirement gathering. The goal was to understand how potential users currently handle meeting discussions and action items, and to collect feedback that would help us shape the core features of our system.
The team was divided into groups based on the type of customer persona being targeted — college-based users and company-based users — while another sub-group focused on preparing documentation and presentation material.

Customer Persona 1 — College (Professors / Faculty)
Nikhil, Naaneeth, and Pavan interacted with professors and faculty members to understand their experience with academic meetings, discussions, and follow-up tasks. They gathered feedback on how action items from faculty meetings are currently tracked, what pain points exist, and how an AI-powered solution could add value in a college setting.

Customer Persona 2 — Corporate (Project Managers / Team Leads)
Rohit and Markandeswara interacted with project managers and team leads from company environments. They focused on understanding how meetings are conducted in professional settings, how action items are assigned and followed up, and what expectations corporate users have from an automated discussion monitoring tool.

Documentation and Presentation
Pranathi and Manasa worked on organizing the gathered information and preparing presentation material — structuring the customer personas, summarizing the feedback, and building slides to visually represent the insights collected from both user groups.

After collecting feedback from both groups, the entire team came together to analyze the responses. We identified the positives — features that users found most valuable — as well as the negatives and limitations they pointed out. This analysis will directly guide our development priorities going into Week 3.
